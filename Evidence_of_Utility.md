# Evidence of Utility

This document records explicit, verifiable evidence that the architecture, governance, or protocols of Foundation Zero have produced measurable value, reduced uncertainty, or successfully constrained systemic degradation. Utility claims MUST NOT be theoretical.

## 1. Governance Utility

### The Constitutional Constraint on Premature Growth
**Date:** [2026-07-14]
**Evidence:** The proposed `P-100: Finding Evaluation` protocol was rejected prior to execution.
**Rationale:** The Constitution's rule that "Informative documents can NEVER modify, extend, or override normative behavior" successfully constrained the project from inventing an "Evidence Governance" layer that lacked empirical justification. 
**Utility Demonstrated:** The governance system demonstrated the capability to restrict its own designers from adding architectural elegance at the expense of evidentiary necessity. 

## 2. Protocol Utility

### Complementary Uncertainty Reduction (P-001 vs P-002)
**Date:** [2026-07-14]
**Evidence:** Execution of `P-001` (Independent Interpretation) exposed ambiguity and undocumented assumptions in the original specification. Execution of `P-002` (Specification Compression, Lineage A) successfully exposed an entirely different class of undocumented normative dependencies (Terminology Broadening / Concrete-to-Abstract Replacement) and identified resilience boundaries that P-001 could not observe.
**Utility Demonstrated:** Execution of P-001 and P-002 produced evidence consistent with the hypothesis that they reduce different dimensions of specification uncertainty. The executed protocols produced reproducible observations under the current experimental conditions, supporting the premise that new protocols are justified only when they target an unaddressed coordinate of uncertainty.
