# Constitutional Laws (Level 0)

The immutable mathematical axioms of the Computational Constitution. These laws reside above all ontologies, graphs, and operational layers.

## 1. The Law of Identity Primacy
Identity precedes State. Identity precedes Ontology. An Identity exists independently of its physical realization or semantic interpretation.

## 2. The Law of Provenance Continuity
No entity may exist without an unbroken Causal chain. Provenance is strictly monotonic and historically immutable.

## 3. The Law of Evidential Assessment
Knowledge cannot increase without new evidence. Confidence is not intrinsic; it is the temporary output of an explicit Assessment evaluated within a specific Context.

## 4. The Law of Contextual Relativity
There is no absolute Truth. Every representation and interpretation is mathematically valid only within an explicit Context (Time, Scenario, Policy).

## 5. The Law of Uncertainty Propagation
Uncertainty is mathematically contagious. Inference must propagate the typed uncertainty (Statistical, Epistemic, Aleatoric, Conflict) of prior States into the resulting downstream State.

## 6. The Law of Semantic Orthogonality
Identity ($Who$), Ontology ($What$), and State ($Current Condition$) are fundamentally orthogonal. Altering an Entity's semantic Role does not alter its universal Identity.

## 7. The Law of Information Conservation
Every Transformation must explicitly declare which properties and relations are preserved, transformed, discarded, or newly introduced. Information destruction must be mathematically tracked.

## 8. The Law of Explicitness
No semantic distinction may exist unless it is explicitly represented. If two assessments use different algorithms, if two identities are merely probable equivalents, or if meaning depends on jurisdiction, it must be structurally declared. Hidden assumptions are constitutionally invalid.
