# Operational Primitives: Intent & Constraints

While Epistemology deals with Belief, Operations deal with Execution. Execution without Intent is merely a workflow.

## 1. The Causal Origin: Intent
Everything operational begins because a conscious agent desires a specific outcome. **Intent** is the causal origin of the Operational Graph.
- `Mission` $\rightarrow$ `Goal` $\rightarrow$ `Intent` $\rightarrow$ `Decision` $\rightarrow$ `Plan` $\rightarrow$ `Execution` $\rightarrow$ `Outcome` $\rightarrow$ `Assessment`

Without explicitly modeling Intent, operational actions lose their "Why."

## 2. Constraint Algebra
Constraints dictate the feasible solution space for executing an Intent. Foundation Zero treats constraints algebraically rather than as isolated objects.

- **Logical Composition**: Constraints can be composed mathematically to define strict feasibility fields.
  - `Feasible_Space = Constraint_A AND Constraint_B AND Constraint_C`
  - Example: `Budget_Max_10k AND Legal_Approval_Required AND Time_Limit_7_Days`.
- **Relaxation**: If a composed space yields no solution, specific constraints can be mathematically relaxed or substituted (`OR`) based on paths defined in the Value Graph.
- **Satisfaction**: An Implementation or Artifact `satisfies` a Constraint Space. This relationship is a First-Class Edge capable of holding an Assessment score.
