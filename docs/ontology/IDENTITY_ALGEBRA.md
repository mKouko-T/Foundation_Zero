# Identity Algebra

Because Identity is independent of State, it is computable. The system relies on strict algebraic operations to manage derivation and continuity.

## Primary Operations
- **Merge**: $(Id_A, Id_B) \rightarrow Id_C$
  Combining two distinct Identities into a new unified Identity.
- **Split**: $Id_A \rightarrow (Id_B, Id_C)$
  Dividing an Identity into multiple discrete components.
- **Fork**: $Id_A \rightarrow Id_{A'}$
  Creating an independent lineage intended for divergent mutation.
- **Compose**: $\{Id_A, Id_B, Id_C\} \rightarrow Id_{System}$
  Building a larger aggregate Identity from components, where components retain their individual Identities.
- **Decompose**: $Id_{System} \rightarrow \{Id_A, Id_B, Id_C\}$
  The inverse of Compose.
- **Aggregate**: $\Sigma(Id_i) \rightarrow Id_{Summary}$
  A lossy combination producing a statistical or summarization Identity.
- **Project**: $Id_A \times Facet_X \rightarrow Id_{Projection}$
  Extracting only a specific dimension or perspective of an Identity.
- **Alias**: $Id_{Alias} \equiv Id_A$
  Declaring a new Identity as functionally identical to an existing one for reference purposes.
