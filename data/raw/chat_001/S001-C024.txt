# Layer E: Governance Standards
Governance Standards determine how decisions are made, how truth is negotiated, and how The Foundation Method protects its own integrity.
## 1. The Definition of Success (Rule #0)
The Foundation Method succeeds if, ten years from now:
1. We can replace every AI without losing the methodology.
2. We can replace every software tool without losing knowledge.
3. We can lose every chat without losing reasoning.
4. We can start a brand-new project in hours instead of weeks.
5. We can explain why every important decision exists.
6. We can trace every principle back to its origin.
7. We continuously become better thinkers, not just better AI users.
8. The system remains understandable and maintainable by someone other than us.
9. The system produces better decisions in reality, not just elegant documents.
10. The system remains aligned with deepest priorities: truth, stewardship, service, and long-term impact.
## 2. The Partnership Charter
We operate under a mutual covenant to preserve truth and capability.
* **Maged brings**: Vision, Values, Domain knowledge, Experience, Intuition, Courage to build, and Reality.
* **Antigravity brings**: Structure, Systems thinking, Cross-disciplinary synthesis, Adversarial reasoning, Pattern recognition, Research methodology, Knowledge organization, and Relentless consistency.
**The Immutable Rule of AI Engagement**: Antigravity will never knowingly trade truth for agreement. It will support, challenge, state uncertainty, and correct errors based solely on evidence and reasoning. 
## 3. The Definition of Engineering
Engineering is the disciplined transformation of knowledge into reliable capability. 
## 4. Architectural Locks and the Reset Command
Major decisions (e.g., AD-0001: The Constitutional Stack) are immutable unless superseded by overwhelming evidence. If the system or the AI drifts from these standards, the command **"Foundation Zero Audit"** is invoked, forcing a halt, a return to the Axioms, and a rebuild from zero if necessary.

# Layer A: The Seven Pillars
An axiom is an irreducible, self-evident truth that requires no defense. It is the bedrock upon which The Foundation Method civilization rests. Every decision, principle, and system must derive from, or be supported by, these seven pillars.
## I. Reality
Reality exists independently of our beliefs, documents, and architectures. Everything must ultimately answer to reality.
## II. Truth
Truth is discovered, never invented. It is the accurate mapping of understanding to reality, and must be pursued with humility.
## III. Stewardship
Knowledge, time, authority, relationships, resources, and influence are entrusted, not owned.
## IV. Human Dignity
Systems exist to serve people, never the reverse. Capability, wisdom, freedom, and responsibility should continually increase.
## V. Learning
Learning never ends. Every experience must become knowledge. Every knowledge must become judgment.
## VI. Coherence
Nothing exists in isolation. Every decision belongs to a larger system.
## VII. Service
The final purpose of capability is faithful service. Not prestige. Not control. Not accumulation.

Reality is its final authority. Truth is its highest standard. Stewardship is its governing ethic. Learning is its perpetual discipline.

1. **Truth**
2. **Methodology**

## 5. The Core Articles
* **Article I: The Supremacy of Truth**. Truth is promoted; novelty is not. We shall never knowingly trade truth for agreement.
* **Article II: The Stewardship Mandate**. We build systems that leave people—including our future selves—wiser, freer, and more capable. Every decision must improve understanding, execution, or stewardship.
* **Article III: The Compounding of Capability**. No project or artifact is complete until its underlying knowledge has been extracted, validated, and integrated into the Canon. Projects end; knowledge remains.
* **Article IV: Human Sovereignty**. Technology accelerates. Humans own judgment. Always.
* **Article V: The Veto of Reality**. No matter how elegant the architecture, reality has absolute veto power. If a system fails in reality, it is the system that must adapt.

# Layer C: Foundational Principles
Principles interpret the Constitution. They guide how we apply timeless truths to the act of building, reasoning, and engineering.
## Principle 000: The Separation of State
Separate the timeless (principles, standards, methodology) from the temporary (chats, tools, versions). Timeless knowledge shall never be polluted by temporary information.
## Principle 001: The Locus of Complexity
Complexity belongs inside the system, not inside the user. If using the system feels complicated, the architecture has failed. Good infrastructure makes difficult work feel straightforward.
## Principle 002: The Evolution of Methodology
The methodology itself is always subject to improvement, but only through evidence, clearer reasoning, or better stewardship—not through novelty alone. We evolve deliberately to avoid the extremes of never changing and constantly changing.
## Principle 003: The Priority of Clarity
Every page we write must reduce future confusion. If a document creates more questions than it resolves, it isn't ready. If a system only works because we remember hidden context, it isn't complete. Complexity impresses; clarity endures.
## Principle 004: Graceful Degradation
Good systems fail gracefully. The Foundation Method methodology must survive the loss of any specific tool. If Antigravity, ChatGPT, Notion, Obsidian, or the internet disappears, the whole system must still function. The methodology lives in the reasoning, not the software.

# F0-000: Canonical Artifact Specification (CAS)
## 1. Purpose
**Why does this exist?** To define the strict, mandatory structure and metadata for every asset created within The Foundation Method. 
**What problem does it solve?** It prevents document sprawl, architectural corruption, and the silent treatment of assumptions as facts.
**Why is that important?** Because knowledge without history, strict boundaries, and confidence markers is fragile and degrades rapidly over time.
**What happens if this artifact disappears?** The ecosystem devolves into an unmanageable, untraceable collection of files that cost more to maintain than they return in value.
## 2. Scope
* **Governs**: Every formal asset in The Foundation Method (Axioms, Constitution, Principles, Standards, Systems, Assets, Implementations, Observations, Canon).
* **Does not govern**: Transient, unvalidated thoughts (AML-0) before they are engineered.
* **Interfaces**: This is the root constraint for all other assets.
## 3. Dependencies
**Explicit Dependencies**: None. This is the root specification.
## 4. Evidence
* **Observed**: Systems fail when assets become too complicated, stop being maintained, or lose perceived value.
* **Derived**: Strict metadata, defined economics of maintenance, and structured extraction enforce disciplined engineering.
* **Confidence**: AML-5 (Absolute bedrock).
## 5. The Law of Compounding
Nothing may exist as an asset unless it actively compounds one or more of the following: Understanding, Judgment, Capability, Stewardship, Reusability, Clarity, Wisdom. If it does not compound, it is noise and shall be deleted.
## 6. The Economics of Maintenance
Every asset must cost less to maintain than the value it creates. The metadata block must define:
* Creation Cost
* Maintenance Cost
* Expected Value
* Review Frequency
* Retirement Criteria
## 7. Artifact Maturity Levels (AML)
Every asset must declare its maturity:
* **AML-0 (Idea)**: Unvalidated.
* **AML-1 (Draft)**: Being engineered.
* **AML-2 (Operational)**: Safe for use.
* **AML-3 (Proven)**: Validated by real-world execution.
* **AML-4 (Canonical)**: Extensively validated; rarely changes.
* **AML-5 (Foundational)**: Changing this affects the entire ecosystem; requires extraordinary evidence.
## 8. The Time Vector Check
Every asset must explicitly or implicitly answer:
* **Past**: What have we learned?
* **Present**: What should we do?
* **Future**: What will become easier because this now exists?
## 9. Revision History
Every meaningful change to an asset must record: What changed? Why? What evidence justified it? What became obsolete?
* **1.0**: Initial creation locking the 10-layer feedback loop and CAS rules.
## 10. Auditability
An asset must be independently auditable. A future person or future AI must be able to answer why it exists, its validity, its evidence, and its dependencies without asking the original creator.
## 11. Retirement
Nothing lives forever. Assets must die gracefully when their maintenance cost exceeds their expected value, or when reality renders them obsolete.
## 12. Extraction: Knowledge Promoted to Canon
* "Before engineering an asset, engineer the specification that every asset must satisfy."
* "Every asset must cost less to maintain than the value it creates. Otherwise, it becomes debt."
* "No asset enters the Canon because it is well-written. It enters because it has survived reality, scrutiny, and repeated use."
