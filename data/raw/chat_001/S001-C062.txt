Strategic Observation
I think we've reached another inflection point.
Initially, Foundation Zero appeared to have three major layers:
•	Foundation
•	Projects
•	Sessions
With the proposed Governance area, the architecture naturally evolves to four orthogonal concerns:
Foundation
    ↓
Governance
    ↓
Projects
    ↓
Runtime State
Notice that Runtime State is orthogonal to Projects, not beneath them. A runtime can operate across multiple projects, while Governance governs them all. I would therefore avoid thinking of Governance as "another folder" and instead treat it as a distinct architectural concern with its own design rules.
I would not canonize this architecture immediately. Like the operating principle, I would record it as an architectural hypothesis to validate through real usage across multiple projects before elevating it to canonical status. That preserves the Constitutional Freeze while allowing the system to evolve based on evidence rather than elegance.
