•	GPT-5

Suppose six months from now you run Source 1 again.
Will you recover the same things?
Or different things?

why?
Because the repository changed?
Because the protocol changed?
Because the AI changed?
Because the prompt changed?
This should eventually become measurable.

One subtle danger:

Sometimes correctly.
Sometimes incorrectly.
Questions worth asking later:

Was it intentionally rejected?
Was it forgotten?
Was it merged?
Was it obsolete?
Was it actually better?
Those are different histories.

Negative evidence is often more valuable than positive evidence.

Not another protocol.
A Thinking Operating System.

For example, before finalizing a recommendation:
1.	Clarify the objective.
2.	Surface assumptions.
3.	Gather evidence.
4.	Generate alternatives.
5.	Actively try to falsify the leading option.

7.	Decide with calibrated confidence.
8.	Define how success will be measured.
9.	Specify what future evidence would change the decision.
