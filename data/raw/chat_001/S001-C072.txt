# Why Now? 
## What has changed in Cornerstone?
Cornerstone has entered a new phase of maturity. We are no longer simply scaling activity; we are scaling complexity. The volume of sales, the intricacies of cross-functional handoffs, the dependence on multiple systems (Odoo, CRM), and the speed at which leadership must execute have all fundamentally increased.
## Why is this becoming harder?
As Cornerstone grows, the cost of fragmented operational visibility increases faster than organizational size. Executive decisions increasingly depend on fragmented information spread across departments, systems, and individuals, making it harder to obtain a timely, trusted, organization-wide view of operational reality. 
This manifests daily:
* Leadership asking the same questions repeatedly because the answers live in silos.
* Information being gathered manually before important decisions.
* Cross-functional issues lacking a single owner for investigation.
* Operational bottlenecks being discovered after they become urgent.
* Improvements depending heavily on key individuals rather than institutional processes.
* A growing, unsustainable reliance on informal knowledge ("tribal knowledge").
## Why existing structures are not designed for this
Current departments (Sales, Finance, Operations) are built to execute their specific functions. They are not designed to measure the friction *between* functions, optimize the data flowing across systems, or provide continuous, unbiased executive decision support. Asking functional leaders to also act as objective systems architects divides their attention and slows down execution.
## Why this proposal exists now
If we do not address this friction, leadership will spend an increasing amount of time assembling information instead of making decisions, and operational excellence will remain reactive rather than systematic. This proposal introduces a targeted mechanism to convert operational reality into clear executive decisions, ensuring Cornerstone scales its execution capability as fast as it scales its revenue.

# Business Case: Establishing a Business Operations Capability
## 1. Value Proposition & Expected ROI
The objective of the Business Operations capability is to act as the shortest path between operational reality and executive decisions. The expected ROI is generated through:
1. **Decision Velocity:** Reducing the latency between an executive question and a trusted, evidence-backed answer.
2. **Operational De-Risking:** Identifying cross-functional bottlenecks (e.g., between Sales and Finance) before they cause revenue delay.
3. **Execution Scalability:** Replacing manual data assembly and "tribal knowledge" with systemic visibility across Odoo and the CRM.
## 2. Risk Analysis (Status Quo)
Failure to institutionalize this capability exposes Cornerstone to escalating friction as it scales.
**Immediate Operational Risks:**
* Slower executive decision cycles.
* Repeated investigation of the same operational issues.
* Inconsistent KPI interpretation across departments.
**Medium-Term Risks:**
* Reduced organizational scalability.
* Lower ROI on CRM/Odoo adoption due to process blindness.
* Improvement initiatives competing without evidence-based prioritization.
**Strategic Risks:**
* Institutional knowledge remains undocumented.
* Leadership spends increasing time assembling information instead of directing strategy.
* Operational excellence becomes permanently reactive.
## 3. Alternative Options
1. **The Status Quo:** Functional leaders continue to act as part-time systems architects. This divides their attention, biases operational analysis, and slows execution.
2. **External Consultants:** High financial cost, temporary impact. Consultants leave with the institutional knowledge they generated.
3. **Internal Capability (Recommended):** Establishes an internal Operational Intelligence nervous system. Retains knowledge as a permanent organizational asset and continuously compounds value.
## 4. Expected Maturity Journey
This capability is not a static department; it evolves with Cornerstone's complexity.
* **Phase 1 (Now):** Business Operations Lead (Pilot focus: Visibility & Bottleneck discovery).
* **Phase 2 (1-3 Years):** Director of Business Operations (Focus: Systems optimization, cross-functional flow).
* **Phase 3 (3-5 Years):** Chief Operating Excellence Officer / Chief Performance Officer (Focus: Enterprise-wide strategic intelligence).
## 5. 90-Day Pilot Success Measures
To de-risk this initiative, the first 90 days will be measured strictly against the following outcomes:
* Reduced time to answer executive operational questions.
* A standardized Executive Operational Brief produced on a regular cadence.
* One or more major cross-functional bottlenecks identified, investigated, and acted upon.
* Improved visibility into CRM and Odoo process performance.
* A prioritized roadmap of operational improvements based on evidence rather than anecdote.
# Business Operations Capability Charter
## 1. Mission
The mission of the Business Operations capability is to act as the central nervous system of Cornerstone's execution. It exists to translate fragmented operational reality into trusted, evidence-backed executive decisions, thereby maximizing operational velocity and reducing organizational friction.
## 2. Core Ownership
The Business Operations Lead owns the architecture of execution visibility, including:
* **Operational Intelligence:** Identifying bottlenecks, friction points, and execution delays across all departments.
* **Executive Decision Support:** Delivering continuous, objective analysis to the COO and leadership team to de-risk strategic decisions.
* **Process Interoperability:** Ensuring smooth, measurable handoffs between Sales, Finance, and Operations.
* **System Alignment:** Monitoring the accuracy and utilization of core systems (Odoo, CRM) to ensure they reflect operational reality.
## 3. Explicit Non-Ownership
To maintain objectivity and analytical capacity, the Business Operations capability explicitly **does not own**:
* **Day-to-Day Operations:** It observes friction; it does not replace the managers running the departments.
* **Direct Sales Quotas:** It optimizes the pipeline; it does not close deals.
* **IT Administration:** It identifies system utilization gaps; it does not perform server maintenance or baseline IT support.
* **Firefighting:** It investigates systemic root causes; it does not permanently absorb temporary workload crises from other departments.
## 4. Authority
To execute this mission, the Business Operations Lead is granted authority to:
* Access all operational data, reports, and system records (Odoo, CRM) without restriction.
* Observe and audit any cross-functional process, meeting, or workflow.
* Propose systemic interventions directly to the COO.
* Require adherence to standardized data entry protocols to ensure operational visibility.
## 5. First 90 Days (Pilot Roadmap)
### Month 1: Reality Mapping
* Identify the top 3 delayed operational decisions currently facing the executive team.
* Map the exact friction points in the Lead-to-Cash process across Sales and Finance.
* Establish baseline metrics for decision latency and system utilization.
### Month 2: Initial Interventions
* Produce the first cycle of Operational Intelligence Briefs targeting observed bottlenecks.
* Align the CRM and Odoo data flows to ensure "one single source of truth" for the executive dashboard.
* Recommend 1-2 targeted process modifications to reduce cross-functional friction.
### Month 3: Institutionalization
* Deliver the 90-Day Pilot Review to the COO.
* Transition successful interventions into standard operating procedures.
* Propose the formal expansion roadmap for the Business Operations capability for the next fiscal year.
