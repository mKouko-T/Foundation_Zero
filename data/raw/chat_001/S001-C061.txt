# DECISION 001: Operational Intelligence Boundary
**Decision ID:** 001
**Date:** 2026-07-07
**Context:** Project Cornerstone Initialization (SESSION 001)
**Context:** Project Cornerstone Initialization (SESSION 001). The scope of Operational Intelligence must be bounded before the first execution to prevent mission creep.
## Statement
Before executing the first intervention, we must explicitly bound the definition of Operational Intelligence to prevent scope expansion and preserve the governing principle that *business outcomes outrank technical outputs*.
## Operational Intelligence IS
**Statement (The Decision):**
Operational Intelligence IS:
* Converting operational reality into decisions that improve execution.
* Identifying where execution deviates from expectations.
* Producing reusable organizational knowledge.
* Measuring improvement.
* Creating evidence executives can trust.
* Closing the loop between observation and action.
## Operational Intelligence IS NOT
Operational Intelligence IS NOT:
* Business Intelligence dashboards.
* CRM administration.
* Odoo implementation.
* AI experimentation.
* Documentation production.
* Reporting for reporting's sake.
* Data warehousing.
* Software architecture.
## Rationale
By defining what it is not, we ensure that Operational Intelligence remains an execution-focused capability rather than devolving into an IT or purely analytical function. This boundary enforces that we change decisions before we change systems.
**Alternatives Considered:**
1. Allowing the capability to organically discover its boundaries without pre-definition.
2. Aligning strictly with "Business Intelligence" definitions.
**Why Rejected:**
1. Organic boundary discovery typically devolves into "doing everything" (PMO, IT, Analytics) until the core capability becomes meaningless.
2. BI focuses on descriptive analytics (what happened), whereas Operational Intelligence focuses on execution (what should we do tomorrow).
**Evidence:**
Historical organizational patterns show that unbounded intelligence initiatives default to becoming dashboard factories or IT administration arms rather than execution levers.
**Expected Consequences:**
* We will reject requests for purely analytical or administrative tasks that do not drive decisions.
* Executive output will strictly recommend action rather than just presenting data.
* We prioritize changing decisions before changing systems.
**Reversal Conditions:**
This boundary will only be reversed or expanded if the organization demonstrates it has fully institutionalized execution-driven intelligence and formally re-mandates the function to absorb downstream capabilities (e.g., assuming ownership of the data warehouse).
