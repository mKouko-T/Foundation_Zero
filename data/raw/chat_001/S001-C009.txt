Cornerstone Business Operations Leadership Track
Function Charter & Role Architecture
Prepared For
Executive Leadership Team
Purpose
Define a high-leverage organizational function that improves operational effectiveness, organizational visibility, decision quality, systems maturity, and scalability while intentionally developing future operational leadership capability.
________________________________________
1. Executive Summary
Cornerstone is entering a new stage of growth characterized by increasing organizational complexity, expanding systems, cross-functional dependencies, operational scale, and decision-making requirements.
To support this growth, the company requires a dedicated function focused on improving organizational effectiveness through performance management, systems optimization, process improvement, operational governance, and executive decision support.
The purpose of this function is not to operate departments, manage daily activities, or absorb operational workload. Its purpose is to strengthen the organization's ability to operate effectively, make better decisions, and scale sustainably.
________________________________________
2. Business Need
As the organization grows, leadership requires:
• Greater operational visibility.
• More reliable information.
• Better cross-functional alignment.
• Stronger process discipline.
• Higher-quality decision support.
• Successful system adoption and utilization.
• Reduced dependence on tribal knowledge.
• Increased organizational scalability.
• Improved execution consistency.
The company needs a function that bridges operations, systems, performance management, process improvement, and executive decision support.
________________________________________
3. Mission Statement
To improve organizational effectiveness by increasing visibility, strengthening systems and processes, enabling reliable performance management, supporting executive decision-making, and building scalable operational capabilities across the company.
________________________________________
4. Core Function Charter
The function exists to improve the effectiveness, visibility, scalability, and decision quality of the organization through:
• Performance management.
• Systems optimization.
• Process improvement.
• Operational governance.
• Executive decision support.
• Continuous improvement.
The function serves as the organizational bridge between leadership, operations, systems, and performance.
________________________________________
5. Core Ownership Areas
A. Organizational Performance
Ownership includes:
• KPI architecture.
• Performance measurement frameworks.
• Executive reporting standards.
• Operational analytics.
• Forecasting support.
• Trend analysis.
• Performance visibility.
Expected Outcome:
Leadership can clearly understand organizational performance and make informed decisions.
________________________________________
B. Operational Excellence
Ownership includes:
• Process mapping.
• Process redesign.
• Bottleneck identification.
• Workflow optimization.
• SOP governance support.
• Continuous improvement initiatives.
Expected Outcome:
Operations become more efficient, repeatable, and scalable.
________________________________________
C. Business Systems Effectiveness
Ownership includes:
• CRM governance.
• CRM adoption.
• CRM process optimization.
• Odoo process optimization.
• Data quality governance.
• Reporting integrity.
• System utilization effectiveness.
Expected Outcome:
Business systems become trusted, adopted, and capable of supporting growth.
________________________________________
D. Strategic Cross-Functional Initiatives
Ownership includes:
• Cross-department projects.
• Improvement initiatives.
• Organizational readiness efforts.
• Change management support.
• Business process integration.
Expected Outcome:
Departments operate more effectively together and organizational friction is reduced.
________________________________________
E. Executive Decision Support
Ownership includes:
• CEO decision support.
• COO decision support.
• CFO decision support.
• Business case development.
• Scenario analysis.
• Root-cause investigations.
• Strategic operational analysis.
Expected Outcome:
Executive decisions become faster, more informed, and better supported.
________________________________________
F. Organizational Learning & Capability Building
Ownership includes:
• Lessons learned frameworks.
• Best-practice documentation.
• Process knowledge capture.
• Improvement knowledge repository.
• Governance documentation.
Expected Outcome:
Knowledge becomes institutional rather than individual.
________________________________________
6. Explicit Non-Ownership
This function is not responsible for:
• Daily administrative follow-up.
• Routine task tracking.
• Meeting scheduling.
• Executive assistant duties.
• Department supervision.
• Attendance management.
• General administration.
• Data entry activities.
• CRM technical administration.
• Odoo technical administration.
• Acting as owner of all operational issues.
• Replacing department managers.
The function improves systems and performance.
It does not absorb operational workload.
________________________________________
7. Decision Rights & Authority
Can Define
• KPI standards.
• Reporting standards.
• Performance measurement methodologies.
• Data governance standards.
• Process documentation standards.
________________________________________
Can Recommend
• Process redesign.
• System enhancements.
• Organizational improvements.
• Operational controls.
• Cross-functional initiatives.
________________________________________
Can Escalate
• Data quality risks.
• Process failures.
• Reporting integrity issues.
• KPI reliability concerns.
• System adoption risks.
• Operational bottlenecks.
________________________________________
Can Access
• Operational data.
• CRM data.
• Performance data.
• Approved financial information required for analysis.
• Cross-functional reporting information.
________________________________________
Cannot Override
• Department heads.
• Financial authorities.
• HR authorities.
• Executive approvals.
• Business owners.
The function influences through visibility, analysis, governance, and recommendations.
________________________________________
8. Success Indicators
The function will be considered successful when:
• Leadership visibility improves.
• Reporting becomes trusted and consistent.
• KPI governance becomes established.
• CRM adoption improves.
• Data quality improves.
• Process consistency improves.
• Bottlenecks are identified and reduced.
• Executive decisions become better informed.
• Cross-functional execution improves.
• Organizational knowledge becomes documented and transferable.
________________________________________
9. Success Boundaries
The function succeeds when it:
• Improves organizational effectiveness.
• Improves decision quality.
• Builds scalable systems.
• Increases organizational capability.
• Strengthens operational maturity.
The function fails when it becomes:
• Administrative.
• Reactive.
• Firefighting-heavy.
• Meeting-driven.
• Person-dependent.
• Focused on activity instead of outcomes.
________________________________________
10. Risk Mitigation Framework
Risk: Becoming the Company's Problem Collector
Mitigation:
Own frameworks, governance, and solutions—not every incident.
________________________________________
Risk: Becoming Executive Administrative Support
Mitigation:
Decision support only.
No executive assistant responsibilities.
________________________________________
Risk: Becoming the Reporting Department
Mitigation:
Every report must support a business decision.
________________________________________
Risk: Becoming a CRM Administrator
Mitigation:
Own governance, adoption, and effectiveness—not administration.
________________________________________
Risk: Becoming a Project Manager for Everything
Mitigation:
Participate in strategic initiatives.
Do not become default owner of all projects.
________________________________________
Risk: Organizational Dependence on One Person
Mitigation:
Build systems, documentation, governance, and transferable knowledge.
________________________________________

Ranked by strategic alignment:

3.	Business Performance Lead
4.	Operations Performance Lead

________________________________________
Final Recommendation
The company should establish a Business Operations Lead function focused on organizational effectiveness, operational excellence, systems maturity, performance management, and executive decision support.
The role should be structured as a capability-building function that improves how the organization operates rather than a position that absorbs operational workload.
Success should be measured by improvements in visibility, decision quality, operational maturity, and organizational scalability rather than activity volume.

Revision 1 — Remove "Executive Decision Support" as a Major Pillar
This is subtle.
I want it present.
I don't want it dominant.
Why?
Because if executives read:
Executive Decision Support
they may unconsciously hear:
Executive Resource
which can slowly become:
Executive Assistant Plus
I would reposition it.
Instead of a major pillar, it becomes a natural outcome of:
•	Performance Management
•	Operational Excellence
•	Systems Governance
________________________________________
Revision 2 — Strengthen Financial Exposure
This is a significant omission.
If the COO truly sees a future operational leadership path, then finance cannot remain adjacent.
Operations leaders who don't understand finance hit a ceiling.
I would add explicit ownership/support around:
Operational Finance
•	Forecasting support
•	Pricing analysis
•	Inventory economics
•	Payment plan impact analysis
•	Operational cost visibility
Not ownership of finance.
Exposure to finance.
Huge difference.
________________________________________
Revision 3 — Add Organizational Design Exposure
This is something future operational leaders need.
Not immediately.
But deliberately.
Examples:
•	Roles & responsibilities clarity
•	Accountability structures
•	Governance models
•	Handoffs between departments
These are leadership muscles.
________________________________________
Revision 4 — Rename "Organizational Learning"
I like the concept.
I don't like the wording.
I would change it to:
Operational Knowledge & Governance
More executive.
Less academic.
________________________________________
Revision 5 — Add One New Success Metric
Currently success is:
•	Better visibility
•	Better systems
•	Better reporting
Good.
But incomplete.
I would add:
Reduced Decision Cycle Time
Meaning:
How long does it take leadership to:
•	Understand a problem
•	Obtain information
•	Make a decision
This is an incredibly powerful metric.
