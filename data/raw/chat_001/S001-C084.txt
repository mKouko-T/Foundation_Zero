### What Exists?
Before defining behavior, we must define the fundamental entities (the "atoms") that exist within any organization:
* **Purpose**: The reason the system exists.
* **Actors**: Entities (Human or AI) capable of sensing, deciding, and acting.
* **Capabilities**: Reusable combinations of resources that execute a specific function.
* **Resources**: The finite inputs (capital, time, attention).
* **Information**: Data that reduces uncertainty.
* **Decisions**: Irrevocable allocations of resources.
* **Constraints**: Hard limits (physics, law, budget).
* **Time**: The asynchronous clocks dictating state changes.
* **Value**: The output recognized by stakeholders.
* **Relationships**: The structural and social connections between actors.
* **Environment**: The external reality containing customers and competitors.
* **Events**: Points in time where state changes occur.
* **Knowledge**: Stored models of reality.
**Central Theorem Hypothesis:** Every organization is an information-processing system whose purpose is to create value through coordinated decisions under constraints.
