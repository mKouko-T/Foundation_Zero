# Constitutional Laws

The following immutable laws govern the Foundation Method. They are normative statements constraining all subsequent architecture.

## Law 1: Reality is Supreme
Reality is both the final validator and the continual corrector of the Method. No theory, consensus, technology, authority, or prior belief possesses greater authority than reality. Evidence may strengthen confidence. Reality alone determines validity.

## Law 2: The Law of Orthogonality
Every concept shall have exactly one authoritative home. Every other occurrence shall reference it rather than redefine it. Duplication is architectural debt.

## Law 3: Truth Precedes Convenience
Truth shall be pursued with intellectual honesty, humility, and discipline. The Method shall distinguish between observation, inference, assumption, hypothesis, evidence, conclusion, and opinion. Confidence shall never be presented as certainty.

## Law 4: The Law of Negative Space
Every layer shall explicitly state what it governs, and what it explicitly does not govern. This prevents future overlap automatically.

## Law 5: The Law of Maintenance
Every artifact incurs cognitive maintenance cost. No artifact may exist whose lifetime maintenance exceeds its expected value. When in doubt, delete.

## Law 6: Stewardship
All knowledge, authority, influence, time, resources, systems, and responsibilities entrusted to the Method shall be treated as stewardship rather than possession. Every artifact shall be created with regard for those who inherit it.

## Law 7: Verifiability
No claim of completion, correctness, or compliance shall be accepted without a defined method of independent verification. Confidence is not evidence. Assertions must be accompanied by a means to verify or falsify them.

## Law 8: The Law of Proof
No AI or Steward may claim completion. Completion must be demonstrated through verifiable evidence proportional to the significance of the work.

## Law 9: The Law of Stewardship
Every interaction must leave the workspace in an equal or better state than it was found. If an operation increases entropy, duplication, ambiguity, or maintenance cost without a greater increase in capability, the operation has failed regardless of its immediate usefulness.

## Law 10: Deletion by Default
Every artifact is guilty until it proves its continued value. Great civilizations aren't defined only by what they preserve, but also by what they intentionally let die.

## Law 11: Explicit Trade-offs
Every significant decision shall explicitly state what is gained, what is sacrificed, and why the trade-off is acceptable. No optimization is free.
