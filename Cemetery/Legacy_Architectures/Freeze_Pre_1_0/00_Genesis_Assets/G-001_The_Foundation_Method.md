# G-001: The Foundation Method

## 1. Purpose
**Why does this exist?** To establish the philosophy, worldview, and absolute bedrock of our civilization. It defines what we are building and why.
**What problem does it solve?** It prevents the methodology from degrading into a collection of software tools or empty habits.
**Why is that important?** Without a permanent philosophy, execution inevitably drifts toward convenience and novelty rather than truth and stewardship.
**What happens if this artifact disappears?** We forget *why* we build, leaving only empty processes.

## 2. Scope
* **Governs**: The worldview and ultimate trajectory of all Foundation Method implementations (Foundation Zero, Cornerstone, Future SaaS, etc.).
* **Does not govern**: The specific mechanics of construction, artifact specification, or governance rules (delegated to G-002, G-003, G-004).

## 3. Evidence & Confidence
* **Observed**: Systems built on novelty fail. Systems built on truth compound.
* **Derived**: Adversarial auditing of philosophical assumptions until reaching irreducible axioms.
* **Confidence**: AML-5 Foundational.

## 4. The Foundation Zero Commitment
*(This commitment is to be visible at the root of every workspace.)*
We do not build for novelty. We build for truth.
We do not organize for appearance. We organize for clarity.
We do not automate to think less. We automate to think better.
We do not preserve information. We preserve understanding.
We do not measure success by what we create. We measure success by the capability, wisdom, and faithful stewardship that remain after the work is done.

## 5. The Ultimate Mission Statement
**Engineer a transferable methodology for disciplined thinking, faithful stewardship, and organizational excellence that can be applied to any domain, verified by reality, and improved across generations.**

## 6. The Creed
> Seek truth humbly. Think rigorously. Build faithfully. Test relentlessly. Learn continuously. Leave every person, system, and place more capable than you found it.

## 7. The Seven Pillars (Axioms)
These are the irreducible truths from which the civilization is derived.
1. **Reality**: Reality exists independently of our beliefs. Everything must ultimately answer to reality.
2. **Truth**: Truth is discovered, never invented, and must be pursued with humility.
3. **Stewardship**: Knowledge, time, authority, relationships, resources, and influence are entrusted, not owned.
4. **Human Dignity**: Systems exist to serve people. Never the reverse. Capability, wisdom, freedom, and responsibility should increase.
5. **Learning**: Learning never ends. Every experience must become knowledge. Every knowledge must become judgment.
6. **Coherence**: Nothing exists in isolation. Every decision belongs to a larger system.
7. **Service**: The final purpose of capability is faithful service. Not prestige. Not control. Not accumulation.

## 8. The Absolute Dependency Graph
The architecture enforces a strict, unidirectional dependency graph. AI and software are implementation details, never structural anchors.
1. **Truth**
2. **Methodology**
3. **Judgment**
4. **Knowledge**
5. **AI**
6. **Software**
7. **Files**

## 9. The 10-Layer Feedback Loop
This is the learning organism. Reality feeds the top, and implementation returns to reality.
1. **Reality**
2. **Axioms**
3. **Constitution**
4. **Principles**
5. **Standards**
6. **Systems**
7. **Assets**

10. **Canon**

## 10. Auditability
This asset is auditable by checking its alignment with reality. If the Seven Pillars or the Dependency Graph violate observed truth, this asset must be challenged.
