# F0-000: Canonical Artifact Specification (CAS)

## 1. Purpose
**Why does this exist?** To define the strict, mandatory structure and metadata for every asset created within The Foundation Method. 
**What problem does it solve?** It prevents document sprawl, architectural corruption, and the silent treatment of assumptions as facts.
**Why is that important?** Because knowledge without history, strict boundaries, and confidence markers is fragile and degrades rapidly over time.
**What happens if this artifact disappears?** The ecosystem devolves into an unmanageable, untraceable collection of files that cost more to maintain than they return in value.

## 2. Scope
* **Governs**: Every formal asset in The Foundation Method (Axioms, Constitution, Principles, Standards, Systems, Assets, Implementations, Observations, Canon).
* **Does not govern**: Transient, unvalidated thoughts (AML-0) before they are engineered.
* **Interfaces**: This is the root constraint for all other assets.

## 3. Dependencies
**Explicit Dependencies**: None. This is the root specification.

## 4. Evidence
* **Observed**: Systems fail when assets become too complicated, stop being maintained, or lose perceived value.
* **Derived**: Strict metadata, defined economics of maintenance, and structured extraction enforce disciplined engineering.
* **Confidence**: AML-5 (Absolute bedrock).

## 5. The Law of Compounding
Nothing may exist as an asset unless it actively compounds one or more of the following: Understanding, Judgment, Capability, Stewardship, Reusability, Clarity, Wisdom. If it does not compound, it is noise and shall be deleted.

## 6. The Economics of Maintenance
Every asset must cost less to maintain than the value it creates. The metadata block must define:
* Creation Cost
* Maintenance Cost
* Expected Value
* Review Frequency
* Retirement Criteria

## 7. Artifact Maturity Levels (AML)
Every asset must declare its maturity:
* **AML-0 (Idea)**: Unvalidated.
* **AML-1 (Draft)**: Being engineered.
* **AML-2 (Operational)**: Safe for use.
* **AML-3 (Proven)**: Validated by real-world execution.
* **AML-4 (Canonical)**: Extensively validated; rarely changes.
* **AML-5 (Foundational)**: Changing this affects the entire ecosystem; requires extraordinary evidence.

## 8. The Time Vector Check
Every asset must explicitly or implicitly answer:
* **Past**: What have we learned?
* **Present**: What should we do?
* **Future**: What will become easier because this now exists?

## 9. Revision History
Every meaningful change to an asset must record: What changed? Why? What evidence justified it? What became obsolete?
* **1.0**: Initial creation locking the 10-layer feedback loop and CAS rules.

## 10. Auditability
An asset must be independently auditable. A future person or future AI must be able to answer why it exists, its validity, its evidence, and its dependencies without asking the original creator.

## 11. Retirement
Nothing lives forever. Assets must die gracefully when their maintenance cost exceeds their expected value, or when reality renders them obsolete.

## 12. Extraction: Knowledge Promoted to Canon
* "Before engineering an asset, engineer the specification that every asset must satisfy."
* "Every asset must cost less to maintain than the value it creates. Otherwise, it becomes debt."
* "No asset enters the Canon because it is well-written. It enters because it has survived reality, scrutiny, and repeated use."
