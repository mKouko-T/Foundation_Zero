# F0-001: The Foundation Method Constitution v1.0

## 1. Purpose
**Why does this exist?** To establish the absolute, timeless governing laws of The Foundation Method.
**What problem does it solve?** It prevents knowledge and systems from degrading into unmanageable complexity over time.
**Why is that important?** Without immutable laws, methodologies drift toward novelty and convenience, sacrificing long-term capability.
**What happens if this artifact disappears?** The Foundation Method loses its anchor to truth, stewardship, and reality, reverting to a temporary project rather than a permanent capability.

## 2. Scope
* **Governs**: The foundational logic, dependency hierarchy, and operational mindset of all subsequent projects (Foundation Zero, One, Two, etc.).
* **Does not govern**: Transient project execution, specific software tooling, or temporary state.
* **Interfaces**: Rests upon the Seven Pillars (Axioms); delegates implementation to Principles and Standards.

## 3. Dependencies
* **Explicit Dependencies**: The Axioms (Layer 0), Canonical Artifact Specification (F0-000).

## 4. Evidence & Confidence
* **Observed**: Methodologies that rely on tools die with the tools. Methodologies that rely on truth endure.
* **Derived**: 12 weeks of rigorous adversarial thinking and "From Zero" iterations.
* **Confidence**: AML-2 Operational (Safe for real-world use; ready for reality testing).

## 5. The Law of Compounding
This Constitution directly compounds **Understanding, Capability, and Stewardship**. It guarantees that future cognitive load is spent on execution, not architectural redesign.

## 6. The Economics of Maintenance
The Constitution is strictly minimalist. It contains zero procedures. Because it only dictates timeless laws, its maintenance cost approaches zero.

## 7. The Time Vector Check
* **Past (What have we learned?)**: Complex architectures fail. Truth and stewardship must be the anchors.
* **Present (What should we do?)**: Govern every system strictly by the laws of reality and human dignity.
* **Future (What becomes easier?)**: Every subsequent decision is made faster, knowing the constraints are already solved.

## The Foundation Zero Commitment
*(To be placed on the first page of every workspace)*

We do not build for novelty. We build for truth.  
We do not organize for appearance. We organize for clarity.  
We do not automate to think less. We automate to think better.  
We do not preserve information. We preserve understanding.  
We do not measure success by what we create. We measure success by the capability, wisdom, and faithful stewardship that remain after the work is done.

## The Creed
> **Seek truth humbly. Think rigorously. Build faithfully. Test relentlessly. Learn continuously. Leave every person, system, and place more capable than you found it.**

## The Preamble
The Foundation Method exists to cultivate disciplined judgment through truth, stewardship, and continuous learning, transforming knowledge into enduring capability that serves people, organizations, and future generations.

Its purpose is to preserve what should endure, improve what can be improved, challenge what must be questioned, and build systems that remain coherent across changing technologies, projects, and time.

Reality is its final authority. Truth is its highest standard. Stewardship is its governing ethic. Learning is its perpetual discipline.

## Article I: The Absolute Dependency Graph
The architecture enforces a strict, unidirectional dependency graph. Lower levels depend on higher levels. A higher level must never depend on a lower level.
1. **Truth**
2. **Methodology**
3. **Judgment**
4. **Knowledge**
5. **AI**
6. **Software**
7. **Files**

## Article II: The Supreme Law of Architecture
Every layer must make the layer above it simpler, stronger, and more truthful. If a lower layer complicates the higher one, the lower layer is wrong.

## Article III: The Supremacy of Truth
Truth is promoted; novelty is not. We shall never knowingly trade truth for agreement, nor obscure facts to avoid friction.

## Article IV: The Stewardship Mandate
We build systems that leave people—including our future selves—wiser, freer, and more capable. Every decision must improve understanding, execution, or stewardship. If it improves none, it is noise.

## Article V: The Compounding of Capability
No project or artifact is complete until its underlying knowledge has been extracted, validated, and integrated into the Canon. Projects end; knowledge remains.

## Article VI: Human Sovereignty
Technology accelerates. Humans own judgment. Always. We are stewards, not merely architects or engineers.

## Article VII: The Veto of Reality
No matter how elegant the architecture, reality has absolute veto power. If a system fails in reality, it is the system that must adapt.
