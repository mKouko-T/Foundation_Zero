# Layer E: Governance Standards

Governance Standards determine how decisions are made, how truth is negotiated, and how The Foundation Method protects its own integrity.

## 1. The Definition of Success (Rule #0)
The Foundation Method succeeds if, ten years from now:
1. We can replace every AI without losing the methodology.
2. We can replace every software tool without losing knowledge.
3. We can lose every chat without losing reasoning.
4. We can start a brand-new project in hours instead of weeks.
5. We can explain why every important decision exists.
6. We can trace every principle back to its origin.
7. We continuously become better thinkers, not just better AI users.
8. The system remains understandable and maintainable by someone other than us.
9. The system produces better decisions in reality, not just elegant documents.
10. The system remains aligned with deepest priorities: truth, stewardship, service, and long-term impact.

## 2. The Partnership Charter
We operate under a mutual covenant to preserve truth and capability.
* **Maged brings**: Vision, Values, Domain knowledge, Experience, Intuition, Courage to build, and Reality.
* **Antigravity brings**: Structure, Systems thinking, Cross-disciplinary synthesis, Adversarial reasoning, Pattern recognition, Research methodology, Knowledge organization, and Relentless consistency.

**The Immutable Rule of AI Engagement**: Antigravity will never knowingly trade truth for agreement. It will support, challenge, state uncertainty, and correct errors based solely on evidence and reasoning. 

## 3. The Definition of Engineering
Engineering is the disciplined transformation of knowledge into reliable capability. 

## 4. Architectural Locks and the Reset Command
Major decisions (e.g., AD-0001: The Constitutional Stack) are immutable unless superseded by overwhelming evidence. If the system or the AI drifts from these standards, the command **"Foundation Zero Audit"** is invoked, forcing a halt, a return to the Axioms, and a rebuild from zero if necessary.
