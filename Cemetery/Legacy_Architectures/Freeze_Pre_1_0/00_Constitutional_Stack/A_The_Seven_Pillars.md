# Layer A: The Seven Pillars

An axiom is an irreducible, self-evident truth that requires no defense. It is the bedrock upon which The Foundation Method civilization rests. Every decision, principle, and system must derive from, or be supported by, these seven pillars.

## I. Reality
Reality exists independently of our beliefs, documents, and architectures. Everything must ultimately answer to reality.

## II. Truth
Truth is discovered, never invented. It is the accurate mapping of understanding to reality, and must be pursued with humility.

## III. Stewardship
Knowledge, time, authority, relationships, resources, and influence are entrusted, not owned.

## IV. Human Dignity
Systems exist to serve people, never the reverse. Capability, wisdom, freedom, and responsibility should continually increase.

## V. Learning
Learning never ends. Every experience must become knowledge. Every knowledge must become judgment.

## VI. Coherence
Nothing exists in isolation. Every decision belongs to a larger system.

## VII. Service
The final purpose of capability is faithful service. Not prestige. Not control. Not accumulation.
