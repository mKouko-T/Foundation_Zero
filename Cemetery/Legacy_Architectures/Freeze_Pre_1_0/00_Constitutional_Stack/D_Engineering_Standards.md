# Layer D: Engineering Standards

Engineering Standards enforce the rules for building, interpreting the Principles into strict architectural mandates.

## 1. The Engineering Lifecycle
Everything built under The Foundation Method must pass through this exact 14-step lifecycle. Projects do not finish; they mature.
1. **Question**
2. **Discovery**
3. **Research**
4. **Model**
5. **Challenge**
6. **From Zero**
7. **Architecture**
8. **Engineering**
9. **Reality**
10. **Measurement**
11. **Knowledge Extraction**
12. **Promotion**
13. **Versioning**
14. **Archive**

## 2. The Six Quality Gates
Every artifact, system, and decision must optimize for these six qualities before acceptance:
1. **Truth**: Factually and logically sound.
2. **Coherence**: Fits the whole architecture.
3. **Stewardship**: Sustainable.
4. **Simplicity**: As simple as possible, but never simpler.
5. **Transferability**: Reusable.
6. **Longevity**: Architected to remain useful years from now.

## 3. Architectural Rules
* **Rule 001 — Nothing Exists Without Purpose**: Every artifact must justify its existence. Merge redundancies.
* **Rule 002 — One Source of Truth**: Every piece of knowledge has exactly one canonical location. Never duplicate.
* **Rule 003 — Layer Isolation**: Constitution never contains procedures. Procedures never contain temporary state. State never changes principles.
* **Rule 004 — Explicit Dependencies**: Nothing depends on hidden knowledge. Every dependency is documented.
* **Rule 005 — Every Layer Must Stand Alone**: Higher layers must be comprehensible without lower layers. Lower layers must be comprehensible without higher layers.
