# F0-999: Foundation Method Validation

**Purpose:** How do we know the Foundation Method itself works? 

This artifact defines how the methodology remains accountable to reality.

## Validation Criteria
The Method succeeds if it produces higher decision quality, reduces repeated mistakes, and enables seamless transferability of knowledge. It fails if it produces documentation without capability, or analysis paralysis without execution.

## Benchmark Projects
The Method must be independently validated against distinct, real-world constraints:
* **The Cornerstone Project** (Deep architectural synthesis)
* **Operations Role** (High-velocity execution and logistics)
* **Future SaaS** (Commercial viability and scaling)
* **Book** (Information structuring and pedagogy)
* **Health/Parenting Systems** (Personal, biological, and stewardship domains)

## Success Metrics
1. **Convergence Velocity**: The time it takes to reach an engineering decision decreases over successive projects.
2. **Maintenance Deflation**: The total volume of governing text decreases as abstractions improve.
3. **Extraction Rate**: 100% of completed projects yield at least one validated insight for the Canon.

## Failure Metrics
1. **Constitutional Bloat**: The Constitution expands with operational rules.
2. **Infinite Recursion**: Time spent organizing the system exceeds time spent building capability.
3. **Duplication Drift**: The same concept requires updates in multiple files simultaneously.

## Review Cadence
* **Annual**: Verification of Success Metrics and Audit of Constitutional Bloat.
* **5-Year**: Longitudinal review of Canon effectiveness and Institutional Memory transferability.

## Sunset Reviews
Every foundational artifact must periodically answer: *If this were proposed today, would we still approve it?* If the answer is not an immediate yes, the artifact must be deleted. This prevents constitutional fossils.
