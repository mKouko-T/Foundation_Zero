# Hypothesis 003: The Four-State Architecture

**Status**: Experimental / Scientific Hypothesis  
**Domain**: Organizational Intelligence Engineering  
**Premise**: A mature engineering discipline must separate the permanent science from the temporary implementation to avoid decades of confusion. We hypothesize that the optimal repository architecture for OIE requires four distinct states.

## The Four States

1. **Science (Discovering Laws)**
   * What is universally true?
   * *Example*: Decision Latency, Organizational Entropy, The 6 Universal Functions.
2. **Engineering (Designing Tools from Laws)**
   * How do we design for it?
   * *Example*: Capability Templates, Taxonomies, Decision Architecture, Capability Genomes.
3. **Reference Architecture (The Ideal Implementation)**
   * What does a correct implementation look like in theory?
   * *Example*: The canonical Lead-to-Cash map, independent of any specific company's software.
4. **Implementation (The Reality)**
   * The actual company running on Monday morning.
   * *Example*: Cornerstone (Reference Implementation #001).

## Validation Protocol
**Do not restructure the current repository yet.**
This architecture remains a hypothesis. If, after months of research and intervention in Cornerstone, we repeatedly find confusion between universal science, reference architectures, and Cornerstone's specific implementation, then we will promote this Four-State Architecture to Foundation and restructure the repository. The repository must obey the empirical discipline it supports.
