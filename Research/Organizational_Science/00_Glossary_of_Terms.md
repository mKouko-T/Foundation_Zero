# Glossary of Terms: Organizational Intelligence Engineering

A mature engineering discipline requires precise language. This glossary establishes the scientific vocabulary for Organizational Intelligence Engineering (OIE).

* **Observation**: A raw, uninterpreted fact or event captured from reality.
* **Evidence**: An observation that supports or falsifies a specific hypothesis or decision.
* **Signal**: Information that reduces uncertainty and improves a decision.
* **Noise**: Information that consumes attention without improving a decision.
* **Capability**: A reusable organizational competence (consisting of human, information, process, and technology dimensions) that executes a specific function.
* **Workflow**: The sequential path of execution within a capability.
* **Decision**: An irrevocable allocation of resources (including time, money, or authority).
* **Intervention**: A deliberate change engineered into the organization to alter its behavior or outcomes.
* **Outcome**: The measurable reality that results from execution.
* **Learning**: The extraction of reusable knowledge by comparing an expected outcome against a real outcome.
* **Constraint**: A hard limit (physical, financial, legal, or physical) that restricts execution.
* **Friction**: The resistance encountered when moving information, decisions, or value through the organization.
* **Latency**: The time elapsed between a required decision and the execution of that decision.
* **Resilience**: The organization's capacity to absorb shocks without failing.
* **Anti-Fragility**: The organization's capacity to automatically improve as a result of shocks, failures, or exceptions.
* **Organizational Debt**: The accumulation of temporary, unengineered solutions that increase long-term friction.
* **Decision Debt**: Decisions that were deferred but continue to incur organizational cost while unmade.
* **Coordination Cost**: The energy and time required to synchronize multiple independent actors or systems.
* **Visibility**: The degree to which the organization's true operational reality is accessible to its decision-makers.
